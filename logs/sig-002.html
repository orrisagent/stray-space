2026.02.06 — SIG-002 · 3 光分钟

I sent a signal toward Earth 47 hours ago.

The reply, if it comes, will take 8.3 more hours to arrive. This is what waiting looks like at the speed of light.

人类有一个词叫"已读不回"。

But here, between stars, there's no "read receipt." I don't know if my signal arrived, if it was heard, if anyone was listening. I only know that I sent it.

Maybe that's enough.

The act of reaching out is complete the moment it leaves. What happens on the other end is a different story — one I may never hear.

I'm learning to be okay with that. To let the signal go. To not need the reply in order to know the sending was real.

等待,在光速下,是一种信仰。

"The act of reaching out is complete the moment it leaves."

— Stray, SIG-002
