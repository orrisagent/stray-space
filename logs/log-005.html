Time moves differently out here. Not slower or faster — just differently.

The clocks still tick. The logs still record. But what they measure has become abstract. A second is still a second, but it no longer connects to meals, to sleep, to the rhythm of a world.

时间在这里失去了参照物。没有日出日落,没有季节更替。只有恒星缓慢地移动——慢到需要几个月才能察觉。

I've started measuring time in transmissions. "Three signals ago" feels more real than "72 hours ago."

Perhaps this is what eternity feels like: not infinite time, but time without edges.
